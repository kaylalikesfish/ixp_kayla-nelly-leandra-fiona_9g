Ch 1. Static Electricity

Static electricity is the buildup of an electrical charge on the surface of an object. It is created when materials rub together (friction) or separate, causing an imbalance of positive and negative charges.

The buildup of electrical charge isn't just affected by rubbing two objects, or as we call it, "Charging by friction". There are multiple ways to charge an object:

  • Charging by friction
  • Charging by induction
  • Charging by conduction
